Dips

Grab the two handles and slowly let your body descend. Once down, push yourself up, utilizing your triceps and chest muscles. Keep your core tight and your knees bent. If you find these too easy or too hard, you could add a resistance band as support or add weighted plates to make it harder.

Push Ups

Begin in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest almost touches the floor, then push back up to the starting position. Keep your core tight throughout the movement.

Plank

Start in a forearm plank position, with your body in a straight line from head to heels. Hold this position, keeping your core engaged and avoiding sagging in your hips or back.

Leg Press

Adjust the seat so that you start off with your legs fully retracted. Adjust the weight to one you feel comfortable with. Push the weight away. Don't lock your knees fully to reduce risk of injury. Be sure to stay seated and don't slide up in order to ensure full quad activation.
